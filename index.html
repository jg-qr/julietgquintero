Selected Projects

Smart Insole for Gait Analysis
2025 · independent research project

I am designing a low-cost smart insole with FSR sensors and a Raspberry Pi to measure how people walk in everyday environments. The system collects plantar pressure data, sends it over Bluetooth to a smartphone, and generates visualizations and logs for later analysis. I am currently testing it with a small group of 3–4 people, and I am adding machine learning features to create gait profiles and send real-time alerts. This project combines my interests in wearable devices, circuit design, signal processing, and making health-related technology more accessible.

Cities & Culture

Mexico City inspires me with its layered history, museums, and neighborhoods—it feels like walking through many eras at once. Boleros and old songs give me a nostalgia for places and times I never lived, yet somehow feel connected to. I used to dream of sitting in a mythical old cantina in Mexico City, listening to boleros echo through the night.

North / Sinaloa

The north, especially its music and sense of identity, means more to me as I grow older. It’s not just that I “got older” and suddenly became nostalgic. It’s that I moved to a completely different city, where the culture feels worlds apart from what I grew up with. And now that the things I once took for granted aren’t “the normal” around me anymore, I appreciate them in a deeper, more conscious way. The music, the accents, the food, the small habits—everything that shaped my idea of home—has become something I value not out of sentimentality, but out of recognition. Distance showed me how much it forms part of who I am.
